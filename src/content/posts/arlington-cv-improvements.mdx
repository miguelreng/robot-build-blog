We are improving the computer vision pipeline used to analyze sidewalk images in Arlington, focusing on increasing the reliability of both image quality validation and defect detection.

The system now applies smarter quality checks using OpenCV, combining blur detection, texture analysis, and edge density to automatically reject images where a sidewalk surface is not clearly visible. This helps reduce noise caused by irrelevant scenes, reflections, or low-quality captures.

For defect detection, we enhanced the pipeline by integrating pretrained deep learning models, including **UNet-based crack segmentation** and **YOLO-based segmentation** models for detecting gaps and surface anomalies. These models are combined with contextual rules to better distinguish real sidewalk defects from patterns caused by pavers, crosswalk paint, shadows, or surrounding vegetation.

> Within the Data Team, we process these results at scale and store them in BigQuery, enabling direct comparison between existing annotations and the new computer vision outputs to measure accuracy and continuously refine the models.
