# Executive Summary

Transitioning from simulation to reality is the 'Holy Grail' of autonomous delivery. In the PI-05 series, we've achieved a significant milestone: reducing navigation-induced latency from 450ms to 85ms while maintaining a 98% sidewalk segmentation accuracy.

## The Simulation Architecture (Isaac Sim)

We rely heavily on NVIDIA Isaac Sim to create high-fidelity digital twins of our deployment areas. In the previous quarter, we generated over 450,000 synthetic sidewalk scenarios, including difficult weather conditions and dynamic obstacles.

> "Our current pipeline randomizes lighting, texture, and occultations to ensure the model doesn't just learn the map, but the fundamental geometry of the urban environment."

## Technical Challenges: Latency vs. Accuracy

The bridge between 'Sim' and 'Real' often breaks at the hardware level. To solve the 0.5 navigation stack, we optimized our UNet-based segmentation to run purely on the robot's local Jetson Orin cluster.

## Performance in the Field

In our latest DC trials, the PI-05 successfully navigated 12.4 miles of sidewalk with zero human interventions. The primary improvement was our new **Dynamic Obstacle Clustering (DOC)**, which treats pedestrians not just as static points but as velocity vectors.

## Infrastructure Stack

*   **Middleware:** ROS 2 (Humble)
*   **Training:** Multi-node PyTorch cluster
*   **Telemetry:** Custom BigQuery ingestion pipeline
*   **Hardware:** PI-05 Custom Chassis
